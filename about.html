About Devasaya

A quiet revival of cloth, colour and conscience.

Devasaya was born in the dunes of Kutch in 2024 — a slow-craft atelier dedicated to reviving the 4,500-year-old art of Ajrakh hand-block printing. We work shoulder-to-shoulder with master karigars from the Khatri community, whose families have practised this craft for ten generations.

Our name — Devasaya — means "an abode of the divine", and we like to believe that every piece of cloth that leaves our atelier carries a little of that quiet, reverent attention.

What we believe

  • That cloth should be made slowly, by hand, in small numbers.
  • That natural dyes are not a luxury but a birthright.
  • That the karigar is not a worker but an artist.
  • That tradition is the most modern thing we have.
  • That what you wear should hold a story.
