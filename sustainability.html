Our Promise

Sustainable by tradition.

Ajrakh has been sustainable for five thousand years — long before the word was fashionable. We simply refuse to compromise it.

Only Natural Dyes

Indigo, madder root, pomegranate rind, harde, alum, iron — every colour at Devasaya comes from the earth. We have never used synthetic or azo dyes, and we never will.

Skin-Safe Cloth

Our natural dyes are non-toxic, hypo-allergenic and gentle even on the softest skin. Devasaya cloth is safe for children, expecting mothers and sensitive wearers.

Fair Artisan Wages

We work directly with master karigars and pay above-market wages. Every piece is signed by the hand that printed it, and our karigars are equity partners in the studio.

Slow, Small Batches

No machine-printing. No factory overruns. We make only what our karigars can hand-print in a season — every Devasaya piece is, by definition, a limited edition.

Water-Conscious

Our wash effluent is filtered through ash, sand and lime beds before returning to the soil — and we collect rainwater for the dye-vats whenever the monsoon permits.

Biodegradable, Always

Cotton, modal silk, natural dye, cotton thread, jute trims. Should a Devasaya piece ever return to the earth, it returns gracefully — no microplastics, no residue.
